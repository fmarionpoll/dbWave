Define amplification settings

This dialog box let you adjust how your data will be acquired and how much amplification is present on each input channel:

  1. Data acquisition card:
    • Upper panel: number of channels, A/D card input mode and miscellaneous information (card name, resolution, encoding mode).
    •  Individual A/D channel settings (table rows 1 to 3): name, channel number and gain .
  2. Amplifier (table rows 4 to 10): name of the amplifier, channel of the amplifier used, gain of this channel, activation or not of a notch filter, IN+ input mode (high-pass filter or GND), IN- input mode (high-pass filter or GND), and low-pass filter. In this example, our data acquisition board was not connected to anything.
  3. Probe (rows 11 to 12): name of the probe and gain of the probe.
  4. The remaining rows (13 to 15) display the resulting gain and sensitivity. Signal max/min (mV) indicates the maximum amplitude that can be recorded by the card on this channel. DT9800 cards can acquire data between +10 to -10 V (without amplification). If the input voltage goes over this limits, the signal is clipped. If you use a total gain of x10, the limits will be +1 V to -1 V. With a gain of x100, it will be +100 to -100 mV. The last row shows how much resolution you have with these settings.

In this example, we have decided to acquire data from 2 channels.

If you hit OK, you return to the previous screen, if you hit "Sampling...", you will adjust the sampling frequency.
