Record signals

In order to record signals, you need to connect your computer to a data acquisition card and to a signal acquisition setup adapted to your preparation.

In order to record from taste or olfactory sensilla, you need to derive signals from a grounded insect with a microelectrode connected to a probe and to an amplifier.

To record data, you will need to define a few parameters:

  • Data acquisition parameters of the data acquisition card:
    • how many channels
    • sampling rate per channel
    • amplification factor on the card (DT cards can amplify signals from x1 to x8) for each channel. Typically: x1.
  • Amplifier settings for each channel:
    • Amplification gain
    • Low pass filter settings (typically 2800 Hz).
    • High pass filter settings (from DC to 10 Hz typically).
  • Pre-amplifier parameters:
    • amplification gain

These parameters will be used to convert the data acquired by your board into real number by a simple formula: voltage = (16 bits number)* 20 V / (A/D gain * amplifier gain * preampli gain).

You will also need to define:

  • during how long data will be recorded and saved on disk
  • how the data acquisition bout should stard (immediately, after pressing a key, or when detecting a TTL pulse)
  • and a few other parameters which describe the experiment and the content of that data acquisition bout.
